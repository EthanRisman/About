    • What is statistical coupling analysis?
    SCA offers the potential to identify secondary residues by identifying correlations between changes in protein sequences. For instance, in a sequence alignment, residues that are not highly conserved may initially seem insignificant. However, by investigating the connectivity of these residues, SCA can reveal interesting relationships between them. pySCA, which is the script that runs SCA, was created by Lockless, Rivoire, Reynolds, and Ranganathan.

    Toy diagram of a SCA read. A Configuration where Asp and Arg form a salt bridge (green). Configuration where Asp and Lys form a salt bridge (blue). B Conservation of either sequence is 50% but coupling dynamics are seen 100% of the time.

    Where can SCA be used?

    pySCA is best used on conserved protein homologs. For example, terpene synthases, transcription factors, or structural proteins. Each species has its own version, and each version will exibit different characteristics.
